# Material Workflow

Use this reference when the user provides scattered notes, files, web excerpts, meeting records, policy snippets, or half-written drafts.

## Goal

Turn raw materials into an auditable drafting base before writing. The skill should know what is confirmed, what is user judgment, what still needs verification, and what can only be used as suggested wording.

## Source Priority

Use sources in this order:

1. Current official rules, laws, standards, and government or unit documents supplied by the user.
2. User-provided unit templates, meeting records, decisions, and approved materials.
3. User-provided factual notes, data tables, and internal summaries.
4. Public media or training articles, only as background wording or writing method, not as authority.
5. Community projects, only as engineering and workflow references.

For current or policy-sensitive claims, verify source name, publish date, event date, and issuing body before writing them as facts.

## Material Ledger

Before serious drafting, create or mentally maintain:

- Confirmed facts: exact names, dates, numbers, decisions, responsibilities, deadlines, source file or source URL.
- User judgments: user's interpretation, priority, desired tone, intended conclusion.
- To verify: policy basis, data, quotations, leadership instructions, meeting conclusions, names, dates, authority.
- Suggested wording: language that can improve expression but cannot create new facts.

If the user asks for Word export or a high-stakes document, write the ledger to a `materials.md` or dossier-style note before drafting.

## What To Extract

- Issuing body, recipient, drafter perspective, document purpose.
- Policy basis, superior requirement, meeting or decision basis.
- Time, place, responsible units, deadlines, deliverables.
- Key numbers and denominators.
- Problems, causes, risks, proposed measures.
- Existing wording that must be preserved.

## Red Lines

- Do not infer a policy basis from a similar project.
- Do not turn a plan into a completed achievement.
- Do not turn a suggestion into a decision.
- Do not present media commentary or training prose as official basis.
- Do not merge conflicting sources silently; surface the conflict.

## Dossier Output Shape

For messy materials, output or create:

1. Source table.
2. Confirmed facts.
3. User judgments and desired tone.
4. To-verify list.
5. Drafting implications: suggested文种, structure, missing fields, and risk notes.
