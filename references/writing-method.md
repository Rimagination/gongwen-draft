# Writing Method

Use this reference for drafting strategy, style, and revision. It summarizes lessons from `秘书工作`, `党建网微平台`, `半月谈`, and related saved materials.

## Start From Intent

公文写作 is受命性、代言性 writing. Before drafting, determine:

- Who is speaking?
- To whom?
- For what official purpose?
- What decision, instruction, request, record, or action should result?
- Which facts and authority support it?

If the user's prompt gives only a topic, produce a skeleton and ask for the missing authority/facts.

## Facts And Views

Use facts to support views; use views to organize facts.

Good facts are:

- true and verifiable
- accurate in perspective and proportion
- typical enough to prove the point
- relevant to the exact issue

Good views are:

- consistent with policy and authority
- grounded in the facts provided
- specific enough to guide action
- not inflated by slogans or vague adjectives

## Avoid 空心公文

Reject writing that is:

- copied from old drafts without current facts
- filled with slogans but missing measures
- full of data without analysis
- grand in title but empty in implementation
- unclear about who does what by when

When revising空心公文, add concrete context, accountable tasks, deadlines, and expected results.

## Handle Leadership Intent

When drafting for a leader or office:

1. Extract explicit instructions from the prompt.
2. Infer only safe, role-consistent intent from context.
3. Separate what is known from what needs confirmation.
4. Translate intent into structure: situation -> judgment -> task -> requirement.
5. Do not write in a personal voice unless the material is explicitly a speech or signed article.

## Practice "无我"

For official documents, hide the author's personal style. Let the issuing body, facts, and official purpose stand in front.

Use:

- 本单位、本机关、我局、我办 only when the issuing subject is known.
- neutral verbs: 印发、通知、请示、报告、商请、函复、研究、部署、推进、落实.
- measured judgments: 基本完成、稳步推进、仍存在、需进一步.

Avoid:

- personal excitement or literary display
- unsupported "重大突破" or "显著成效"
- overuse of four-character slogans and symmetrical headings
- mixing internet tone into official materials

## Revision Loop

Use this loop:

1. 文种: Is the document type right?
2. 事由: Is the main matter clear?
3. 事实: Are facts complete and reliable?
4. 结构: Does each section do a distinct job?
5. 措施: Are actions executable?
6. 语言: Is it accurate, concise, and official?
7. 风险: Any confidentiality, authority, legal, or political risk?

## Heading Patterns

Use headings to clarify logic, not decorate.

Common safe patterns:

- "一、基本情况"
- "二、主要问题"
- "三、工作安排"
- "四、有关要求"
- "一是... 二是... 三是..." only when parallel items are truly parallel.

Avoid forcing ornate paired headings when content is operational.
