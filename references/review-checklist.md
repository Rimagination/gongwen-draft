# Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing, polishing, or approving a draft.

## A. Necessity And Authority

- Is a document necessary?
- Is the issuing body authorized to issue this content?
- Does the document exceed authority, promise unavailable resources, or decide matters needing higher approval?
- Is the policy/legal basis current and accurately named?

## B. Document Type

- Does the文种 match the purpose?
- 请示: one matter, specific request, no report-only content.
- 报告: no request for approval.
- 函: used for non-subordinate consultation/request/reply.
- 纪要: records agreed matters, not post-hoc invention.
- 通知: requirements and responsibilities are clear.

## C. Structure

- Title states issuing matter and文种 clearly.
- 主送机关 is appropriate.
- Opening explains purpose/basis without overlong background.
- Body follows a clear logic: background -> issue -> task/request -> requirements.
- Each section has one job and avoids repetition.
- Ending matches文种: "妥否，请批示", "特此报告", "此复", "请予支持", etc.

## D. Facts And Data

- Names of agencies, people, places, policies, meetings, and dates are exact.
- Numbers are internally consistent.
- Facts are current and relevant.
- No invented achievements, vague statistics, or unsupported judgments.
- Problems are stated honestly and proportionately.

## E. Actionability

- Requirements identify responsible units or audiences.
- Deadlines, standards, reporting methods, and expected outputs are clear when needed.
- Measures are feasible and within authority.
- Requests are concrete enough for approval or reply.

## F. Language

- Accurate, plain, and official.
- No internet slang, emotional exaggeration, or literary self-display.
- Avoid redundant openings, hollow slogans, and forced parallelism.
- Terms are consistent: e.g. "制定/制订", "截至/截止", "权利/权力", "受权/授权".
- Punctuation follows `references/punctuation-style.md`: Chinese full-width marks in Chinese prose, `：` not `:`, `“”` not `" "`, `一、（一）1.（1）` hierarchy, `附件：` in descriptions, and no unnecessary顿号 between parallel quoted/book-title-marked items.
- Numbers and abbreviations follow formal Chinese writing conventions.

## G. Confidentiality And Risk

- Remove secrets, internal deliberation details, personal identifiers, and sensitive data unless explicitly allowed.
- Mark placeholders instead of guessing confidential facts.
- Do not include seals, signatures, document numbers, or approval language unless provided.

## Output Review Format

For reviews, respond with:

1. Overall judgment: usable / needs revision / high risk.
2. Key issues ordered by severity.
3. Revised text or targeted edit suggestions.
4. Facts needing confirmation.
